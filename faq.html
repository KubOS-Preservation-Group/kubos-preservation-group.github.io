The Community

How did the name "KubOS Preservation Group" come about?

The name KubOS Preservation Group is inspired by Repair Preservation Group, a nonprofit organisation that is creating resources and spreading a love for repair by creating community resources and events.

What is the story behind the banner image on the homepage?

Back in 2017, the then-CEO of KubOS did an interview where he talked about a cubesat that he had been holding and working on. A few months later, a series of pictures of this very cubesat being launched. One of those very NASA pictures is currently being used as the background image for this website.

We are hoping to be able to post a full version of this story over on our blog in the future.

How can I get involved with the community?

The main way to get involved is by joining our discord server. This is the main place where you can chat with other KubOS users and learn more about the community. There are channels to help you get started and learn more about the various projects that people are working on.

You can also follow our repositories on GitHub. If you would like to contribute, check out the issues that are labelled "good first issue" for a place to get started. Contributions to improve the KubOS documentation are also a great, beginner friendly way to contribute while also learning more about KubOS.
